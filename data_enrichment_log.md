## Why these additions

The Task 1 exploration notebook (`notebooks/task1_exploration.ipynb`) found
that most indicators had only 1–3 data points, and that the `DEPTH`,
`QUALITY`, and `TRUST` pillars had zero observations. Enrichment targeted
three things, informed by the provided `Additional_Data_Points_Guide.xlsx`:
giving existing thin indicators more history, adding a first `DEPTH`-pillar
data point, and adding real events/impact_links the starter data didn't
capture (regulatory directives, an infrastructure milestone, and a gender
policy initiative).

## New Observations

### REC_0034 — Mobile Money Agent Count
- **Pillar / Indicator:** ACCESS / `ACC_MM_AGENTS`
- **Value:** 200,000 agents, 2022-09-30, all/national
- **Source:** GSMA / FSD Ethiopia, "Mobile Money in Ethiopia" report — https://fsdethiopia.org/wp-content/pdf/Financial%20Sector%20Deepening%20Ethiopia%20Blog%20_%20Mobile%20Money%20in%20Ethiopia.pdf
- **Original text:** *"mobile money agents grew by 200% in the year to September 2022 to over 200,000"* (figure attributed by GSMA/FSDE to NBE data)
- **Confidence:** medium (secondary source synthesizing NBE data, not NBE's own report directly)
- **Why useful:** Agent network density is called out as a direct correlate of financial access in the provided enrichment guide (item B.3); the starter data had no agent-count indicator at all, despite agents being the primary access point for mobile money in rural/underbanked areas.

### REC_0035 — Telebirr Agent Count
- **Pillar / Indicator:** ACCESS / `ACC_TELEBIRR_AGENTS`
- **Value:** 111,000 agents, 2023-07-01, all/national
- **Source:** same GSMA/FSD Ethiopia report as above
- **Original text:** *"incumbent Telebirr has 111,000+ agents with a 20% active rate on a weekly basis"*
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Why useful:** Provider-level detail complementing REC_0034; the 20% weekly active rate (noted in `notes`, not separately modeled) flags an agent-quality gap — a large network with low utilization — worth tracking distinctly from raw counts.

### REC_0036 / REC_0037 — Mobile Money Awareness Rate (male / female)
- **Pillar / Indicator:** GENDER / `GEN_MM_AWARENESS`
- **Values:** 49% (male), 35% (female), 2023-06-30, national
- **Source:** GSMA survey fieldwork in Ethiopia, cited in the FSD Ethiopia report — same URL as above
- **Original text:** *"only 35% of female respondents reported being aware of mobile money compared to 49% of male respondents"*
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Why useful:** A new GENDER indicator distinct from the existing `GEN_GAP_ACC` (account-ownership gap): awareness is an *upstream* barrier before account opening even becomes possible, and this 14pp awareness gap plausibly helps explain the downstream ownership gap already tracked.

### REC_0038 — Telebirr Cumulative Microloans Disbursed
- **Pillar / Indicator:** DEPTH / `DEPTH_TELEBIRR_LOANS`
- **Value:** 1,000,000 loans (cumulative), 2023-07-01, national
- **Source:** same GSMA/FSD Ethiopia report
- **Original text:** *"Telebirr states that it has disbursed 1million+ microloans"*
- **Confidence:** medium (provider-reported, approximate figure — "1 million+")
- **Why useful:** The **first DEPTH-pillar observation in the entire dataset**. Savings, credit, and insurance ("beyond payments") were completely uncovered; this gives the forecasting task at least one real starting point for that pillar.

### REC_0039 / REC_0040 — Internet Penetration Rate (Jan 2023 / Jan 2024)
- **Pillar / Indicator:** ACCESS / `ACC_INTERNET_PEN`
- **Values:** 16.7% (2023-01-15), 19.4% (2024-01-15), national
- **Sources:** Freedom House *Freedom on the Net 2024* (citing DataReportal/ITU) — https://freedomhouse.org/country/ethiopia/freedom-net/2024 ; DataReportal *Digital 2024: Ethiopia* — https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2024-ethiopia

## Notes on methodology and honesty

- Every added record has a real, checkable `source_url` and an exact quote in
  `original_text` — nothing here was fabricated or extrapolated without a
  citation.
- Where a source used approximate language ("nearly two-thirds", "1 million+"),
  the record's `confidence` was set to `medium`, not `high`, even when the
  source itself (e.g., NBE) is a primary regulator — the imprecision belongs
  to the *figure*, not the *source's* trustworthiness, but both matter for
  confidence.
- New impact_links follow the starter data's own convention of distinguishing
  `direct` (event directly causes the change), `indirect` (via an
  intermediate mechanism), and `enabling` (creates conditions for future
  change) relationships, and of using `evidence_basis: theoretical` (with an
  optional `comparable_country`) rather than `empirical` whenever the
  Ethiopia-specific outcome hasn't actually been observed yet.
- As with the starter data, **every event-to-indicator link here should be
  read as a plausible, sourced hypothesis, not a proven causal effect** —
  this is consistent with the correlation-vs-causation framing used
  throughout the rest of this project.

## Data quality finding in the starter data (not corrected, flagged only)

While writing validation tests, I found that **5 starter-data observations**
are missing `source_url`: `REC_0013` (Fayda Digital ID Enrollment, World
Bank), `REC_0020` (P2P/ATM Crossover Ratio), `REC_0023` (M-Pesa Registered
Users, Safaricom Results), `REC_0024` (M-Pesa 90-Day Active Users, Safaricom
Results), and `REC_0025` (Mobile Money Activity Rate). The two "Calculated"
records (`REC_0020`, `REC_0025`) are derived ratios with no single external
URL to cite, which is understandable. The other three (Fayda ID, M-Pesa
registered/active users) plausibly do have a citable source (World Bank ID4D
pages, Safaricom investor results) that simply wasn't filled in. I did not
retroactively add these — it would risk guessing at a URL the original
collector didn't intend — but flagging this here as a good candidate for a
future correction pass, consistent with this task's "additions **and
corrections**" deliverable wording.
